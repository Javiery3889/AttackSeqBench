3. Lateral Movement: This tactic is used to move from the initial compromised system to other systems within the network. It's usually used after Discovery to expand the attacker's foothold.

4. Execution: This tactic involves running commands or scripts to achieve the attacker's goals. It can be used at any stage of the attack, but it's often used after Lateral Movement to install malware or perform other actions on the targeted systems.
