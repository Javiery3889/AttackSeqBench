   - **Privilege Escalation:** This involves gaining higher-level permissions on the system, which can be used to spread to other systems or achieve strategic goals.
   - **Strategic Goals:** These are the ultimate objectives of the attack, such as data exfiltration, lateral movement, or system disruption.

   - **Logical Sequence:**
     - **Initial Compromise:** The attacker gains initial access and executes malicious code.

     - **Privilege Escalation:** Once persistence is established, the attacker can then focus on gaining higher privileges. This is necessary to perform more advanced actions, such as moving laterally within the network or accessing sensitive data.
     - **Strategic Goals:** With persistence and elevated privileges, the attacker can then work towards achieving their strategic goals.

   - **Why Persistence First?**
     - **Resilience:** Ensuring persistence makes the attack more resilient to disruptions, such as system reboots or security patches.
     - **Stealth:** Persistence mechanisms can be designed to be stealthy, making it harder for defenders to detect and remove the attacker.
     - **Control:** Having persistence allows the attacker to control the compromised system over a longer period, which is essential for more complex and prolonged attacks.
