- **Reconnaissance Phase**: The use of `adfind` and `PowerSploit` indicates that the attacker has already gathered domain information.
- **Initial Access**: Before executing malicious binaries, the attacker needs to gain initial access to the system. Both phishing (T1566) and drive-by compromise (T1189) are common methods for gaining initial access.
- **Execution Phase**: Once initial access is gained, the attacker can then execute malicious binaries using command and scripting interpreters.

Given the context, both phishing and drive-by compromise are plausible methods for gaining initial access. However, phishing is a more common and direct method for delivering malicious payloads that can lead to the execution of binaries.
