**Command and Control (C2):** This is when malware communicates with its operators or with other malware. It's like the malware calling home to report what it's done or to get new instructions.

**Discovery:** This is when the malware is trying to figure out its environment. Like, what's on the machine, what other machines are on the network, that sort of thing.

**Persistence:** This is when the malware tries to ensure that it keeps running even after the machine is restarted or the user logs out.

**Collection:** This is when the malware gathers information that it's been instructed to collect, like stealing files or monitoring keystrokes.

Persistence is about ensuring the malware continues to run over time.

Collection is about gathering specific information as per the attacker's goals.
