- **Lateral Movement** is when attackers move within a network to access other systems or resources, often to spread their influence or reach a specific target.

- **Discovery** involves attackers gathering information about the network, systems, and applications to plan their next moves.

- **Command and Control** (C2) is the communication between the attacker's malware and the compromised systems to control them and extract data.

- **Exfiltration** is the final stage where attackers steal the data they've gathered.

So, in this context, the tactic that involves using a domain to monitor and control infected computers is clearly Command and Control.

But let's double-check the other options to make sure.

- **Lateral Movement**: While lateral movement might involve communication between systems, it's more about moving within the network to access other parts, not specifically about controlling infected computers from an external location.

- **Discovery**: This is about gathering information, not about controlling systems.

- **Exfiltration**: This is about stealing data, which happens after controlling the systems and gathering the data.

Therefore, the most fitting tactic here is Command and Control.
