Let's look at what each of these tactics entails according to the MITRE ATT&CK framework.

**Persistence** is about an adversary ensuring they can maintain access to a system across restarts, updates, or other events that might otherwise disrupt their access. This could involve creating new user accounts, modifying system settings, or installing backdoors.

**Discovery** involves the adversary trying to understand the environment they've infiltrated. This includes mapping the network, identifying users, systems, and applications, and gathering other information that can help them plan their next moves.

**Lateral Movement** is when an adversary moves within the network from one system to another, often to access more sensitive data or to establish a better foothold in the environment.

**Resource Development** is about an adversary acquiring resources, such as money, materials, or infrastructure, to support their operations. This is more common in the preparatory stages of an attack.

Given these definitions, let's map them back to the actions described in the question.

The initial actions—using "net user" and "systeminfo.exe" to collect information—seem squarely in the **Discovery** phase. They're gathering intelligence about the environment.

Then, using compromised privileged accounts to traverse the network suggests they're moving from one system to another, which aligns with **Lateral Movement**.
