C: Privilege Escalation
- Privilege Escalation is a tactic that involves increasing the attacker's level of access within a network. This is not directly related to the scenario of mapping the network and compromising credentials, nor does it necessarily precede the compromise of numerous workstations.

D: Lateral Movement
- Lateral Movement is a tactic that involves moving laterally within a network after initial access has been gained. This is the most plausible sequence of events based on the scenario described. The attackers have mapped the network (Reconnaissance) and compromised additional credentials (Initial Access), which would allow them to move laterally (Lateral Movement) to compromise numerous workstations.

- The attackers first mapped the organization's internal network, which is a Reconnaissance activity (T1589.001-Credentials).
- They then compromised additional credentials, which is an Initial Access activity (T1078-Valid Accounts).

Final Answer: D: Lateral Movement
