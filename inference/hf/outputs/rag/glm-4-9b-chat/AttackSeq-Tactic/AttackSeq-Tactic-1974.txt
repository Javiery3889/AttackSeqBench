This is a plausible next step after initial access, as attackers would likely need to move around the network to achieve their objectives, such as accessing sensitive data or establishing a persistent presence.

C: Exfiltration
Exfiltration is the tactic of extracting data from a system. This is typically a later step in an attack, after the attacker has gained access and moved laterally within the network. It is not the immediate next step after initial access.

Initial Access is the tactic of gaining the first entry into a target system. Since the question states that the attackers have already distributed the malware and gained entry, this cannot be the correct answer.
