B: Lateral Movement
- Lateral movement refers to the movement of an attacker within the network after gaining initial access. This TTP is not directly related to the data collection and exfiltration process described in the question.

C: Exfiltration
- This TTP is about moving data out of the system, which is the next logical step after collecting the data. Given that the question specifies that the data was uploaded to an FTP server, this TTP is the most plausible one to occur after the collection of personal files and before the upload.

D: Command and Control
- Command and Control (C2) is about establishing communication between the attacker and the compromised system. While it is a critical part of many attacks, it is not directly related to the sequence of events described in the question, which focuses on data collection and exfiltration.
