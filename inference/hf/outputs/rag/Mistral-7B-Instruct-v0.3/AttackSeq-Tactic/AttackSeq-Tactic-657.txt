4. Exploiting vulnerabilities on internet-facing servers (T1190-Exploit Public-Facing Application) is a part of the Initial Access tactic and does not logically follow the sequence of events after credential harvesting and network reconnaissance.
5. Privilege Escalation (T1078) and Resource Development (T1135) are tactics that occur after an attacker has gained a foothold in the network and is looking to escalate privileges or establish persistence, respectively. Since we are looking for the tactic that most likely occurred after credential harvesting but before exploiting internet-facing servers, neither of these tactics is the best fit for this sequence.
6. Lateral Movement (T1021, T1041, etc.) is the tactic that best fits the sequence of events, as it describes the movement of an attacker within a network after initial access has been gained and before they attempt to exploit internet-facing servers.
