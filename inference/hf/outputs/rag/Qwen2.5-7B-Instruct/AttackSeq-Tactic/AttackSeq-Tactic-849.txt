  - Exfiltration is the process of transferring data from a compromised system to an external location. This is usually a later step in the attack lifecycle, after the attacker has established a foothold and has gathered the necessary information.

  - Persistence is the tactic used to maintain access to a system over a long period. This typically occurs after initial access has been achieved and the attacker has established a foothold.
