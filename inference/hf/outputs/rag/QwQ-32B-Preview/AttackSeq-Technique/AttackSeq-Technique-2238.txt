A: T1110.004 - Credential Stuffing

Credential stuffing is when attackers take stolen credentials from one service and use them to try to log in to other services. So, if they have usernames and passwords from one place, they try those same credentials on other sites to see if users have reused their passwords.

Given that they've already used a keylogger to get some credentials, it makes sense that they might try credential stuffing to gain access to additional accounts on different services.

But, is this what happens before the password resets?

Well, maybe. If they use credential stuffing to gain access to more accounts, they might then want to secure those accounts by resetting passwords to lock out the legitimate users.

So, this could be a plausible step before attempting to overwhelm targets with password resets.

B: T1105 - Ingress Tool Transfer

Ingress tool transfer is when attackers move tools or scripts from one system to another to perform their objectives.

In this context, maybe they transfer a tool to perform the password resets.

But, does this directly lead to the password resets?

It's possible, but it seems a bit indirect. Transferring a tool is more of a preparatory step, whereas credential stuffing is more directly related to gaining access.

So, while this could be part of the sequence, it doesn't seem as directly connected to the password resets as credential stuffing is.

C: T1137.005 - Outlook Rules

Outlook Rules might be used to manipulate email settings or divert emails, but it's not immediately clear how this fits into the sequence between keylogging and password resets.

It might be used for maintaining access or exfiltrating data, but it doesn't seem directly related to the act of resetting passwords to overwhelm the target.

So, this seems less likely.

D: T1098 - Account Manipulation

Account manipulation involves changing account settings to maintain access or prevent detection.

This could include changing passwords, adding new credentials, or modifying account properties.

Given that the next step is repeatedly attempting password resets, account manipulation seems very relevant.

In fact, resetting passwords is a form of account manipulation.

So, this seems like a direct precursor to the password reset attempts.
