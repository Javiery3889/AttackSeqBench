Let's think about the sequence of events in a typical cyber attack.

1. **Reconnaissance**: The threat actor gathers intelligence about the target to identify potential entry points.

2. **Initial Access**: Gaining entry into the target network, often through phishing, exploits, etc.

3. **Execution**: Running malicious code on the target system.

4. **Persistence**: Ensuring they can maintain access over time, even if credentials change or systems are rebooted.

5. **Privilege Escalation**: Gaining higher-level permissions to access more sensitive data or systems.

6. **Defense Evasion**: Techniques used to avoid detection by security solutions.

7. **Credential Access**: Stealing credentials to gain broader access or to access specific systems.

8. **Discovery**: Mapping out the network to understand the environment and identify valuable data.

9. **Lateral Movement**: Moving across the network to access other systems or data.

10. **Collection**: Gathering the data of interest.

11. **Exfiltration**: Transmitting the stolen data out of the network.

Given this sequence, the collection and exfiltration steps come towards the end of the attack chain. So, the tactic that likely occurred before these steps would be something that sets up the collection, such as lateral movement to access more systems containing data, or credential access to get permissions to sensitive areas.
