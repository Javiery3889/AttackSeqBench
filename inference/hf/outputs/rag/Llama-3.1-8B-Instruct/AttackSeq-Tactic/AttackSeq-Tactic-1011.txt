- The other related TTP is Exfiltration (Tactic) with Exfiltration Over C2 Channel (T1041) as a technique. This indicates that the attackers are transferring data to a C&C server.

- Lateral Movement (B) refers to the movement of the attacker within the network, which is not directly related to the transfer of data to the C&C server.
- Exfiltration (C) is the tactic of transferring data out of the compromised network, which aligns with the transfer of collected data to the C&C server.
- Persistence (D) refers to the attacker maintaining access to the compromised system, which is not directly related to the transfer of data to the C&C server.
