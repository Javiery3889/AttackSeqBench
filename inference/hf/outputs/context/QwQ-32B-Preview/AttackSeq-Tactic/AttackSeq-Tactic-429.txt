In the MITRE ATT&CK framework, these activities fall under different tactics.

Reconnaissance is about gathering information about the target before the actual attack. This could include identifying potential entry points, understanding the network topology, and so on.

Initial Access is the stage where the attackers gain their first foothold into the target network, often by exploiting vulnerabilities or using credentials to log in.

Command and Control is about maintaining communication with the compromised systems to control them and exfiltrate data.

Discovery is about the attackers understanding the environment once they're in, enumerating systems, users, and permissions.
