3. **Persistence**: The attacker might then implement mechanisms to maintain access to the system, even after restarts or other interruptions.

4. **Privilege Escalation**: If necessary, the attacker may attempt to gain higher privileges on the system to perform more actions.

5. **Defense Evasion**: The attacker uses techniques to avoid detection by security solutions.

6. **Credential Access**: The attacker may attempt to steal credentials to access other systems or resources.

7. **Discovery**: The attacker gathers information about the system and network to plan further actions.

8. **Lateral Movement**: The attacker moves across the network to access other systems or resources.

9. **Collection**: The attacker gathers the data they are interested in.

10. **Exfiltration**: The attacker transfers the collected data out of the network.

11. **Command and Control**: The attacker maintains communication with the compromised system to control it and receive data.
