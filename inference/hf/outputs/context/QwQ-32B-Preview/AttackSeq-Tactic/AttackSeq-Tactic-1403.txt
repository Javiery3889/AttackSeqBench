- sysCrt32.exe checks for the presence of these modifications and calls scripts to add them if they're missing.

This suggests that after gaining initial access, the attackers want to ensure they can maintain access over time.

This indicates that once access is established and persistence is set, the attackers move on to stealing credentials.

- libCache32.exe and libTemp32.exe are involved in keylogging and clipboard content logging.

So, after accessing credentials, the attackers proceed to gather more data from the system.

Command and Control:

This suggests that the attackers need to maintain communication with the infected system to control it and retrieve data.

Finally, Exfiltration:

This is the stage where the stolen data is sent back to the attackers.
