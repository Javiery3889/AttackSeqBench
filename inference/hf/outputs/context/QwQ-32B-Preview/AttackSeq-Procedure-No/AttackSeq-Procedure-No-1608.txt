I need to determine whether SideCopy is likely to gather encryption keys from Azure AD services before targeting Kavach.

First, I need to understand what Azure AD services like ADSync and Active Directory Federated Services are. Azure AD is Microsoft's cloud-based directory and identity management service. ADSync (Azure AD Connect) is used to synchronize on-premises directories with Azure AD. Active Directory Federated Services (AD FS) is used for federated identity management, allowing users to access resources across different domains.

Encryption keys in this context likely refer to keys used for encrypting data or for authentication purposes in these services.

The question is asking if, before targeting Kavach, SideCopy is likely to gather such encryption keys to establish initial access to victim systems.

Looking back at the CTI outline:

- SideCopy targets government personnel in India, especially military personnel.

- They are interested in credential harvesting, particularly targeting Kavach.

- Their infection methods include malicious LNK files, HTAs, loader DLLs, self-extracting RAR files, and MSI installers.

- They have developed specific malware and plugins for espionage, including file enumerators and credential stealers.

- Their command and control infrastructure is set up to serve malicious artifacts to specific geographies: India and Pakistan.

Nowhere in the outline is there a mention of SideCopy attempting to gather encryption keys from Azure AD services like ADSync or AD FS servers.

Given that their primary method of initial access is through phishing and malicious files, it seems they rely on social engineering and exploiting user vulnerabilities rather than targeting infrastructure-level encryption keys.

Moreover, their focus is on credential harvesting, particularly from end-users through Kavach and webmail credentials. Targeting Azure AD encryption keys would be a more sophisticated and infrastructure-focused approach, which isn't indicated in the outline.

Therefore, based on the information provided, it's unlikely that SideCopy is gathering encryption keys from Azure AD services to establish initial access.
