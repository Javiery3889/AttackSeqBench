### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- APT10 used DLL Side-Loading to load a malicious DLL (jli.dll) using a legitimate executable (jjs.exe).
- This method was used to inject a shellcode into svchost.exe.

**Execution:**
- The malicious DLL (jli.dll) mapped svchost.bin to memory and decrypted it, revealing the shellcode.
- The shellcode was injected into svchost.exe.
- The injection process involved creating a process in a suspended state, allocating memory, writing the shellcode, and executing it remotely.

**Defense Evasion:**
- APT10's loader used a decryption and injection mechanism to evade defenses.
- The PlugX payload replaced the DOS and NT headers' magic values with 'VX' to prevent detection.
- Dummy calls to GetForegroundWindow API function were used to generate noise and evade detection.

**Command and Control:**
- APT10 used C&C servers located in South Korea.
- The domains used for C&C were registered in Hong Kong and resolved to IP addresses associated with other domains used by APT10.

**Others:**
- APT10 delivered different payloads, including PlugX and Quasar RATs.
- The group used typosquatting domain names and targeted Southeast Asia.
- The threat actor is still active and may be testing or conducting short-lived attacks.
