### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- APT10 used DLL Side-Loading to load a malicious DLL (jli.dll) using a legitimate executable (jjs.exe).
- The malicious DLL injected a shellcode into svchost.exe.

**Execution:**
- The malicious DLL mapped svchost.bin to memory, decrypted it, and injected the shellcode into svchost.exe.
- The process involved creating a suspended process, allocating memory, writing the shellcode, and executing it remotely.

**Defense Evasion:**
- The loader used a decryption and injection mechanism to evade defenses.
- The PlugX payload replaced DOS and NT headers' magic values with 'VX' to prevent detection.
- Dummy calls to GetForegroundWindow API were used to generate noise and evade detection.

**Command and Control:**
- APT10 used C&C servers located in South Korea.
- The domains used for C&C were registered in Hong Kong and resolved to IP addresses associated with DAOU TECHNOLOGY in South Korea.

**Others:**
- APT10 delivered payloads like PlugX and Quasar RATs.
- The group used typosquatting domain names and targeted Southeast Asia.
- The threat actor is still active and may be testing new techniques.
