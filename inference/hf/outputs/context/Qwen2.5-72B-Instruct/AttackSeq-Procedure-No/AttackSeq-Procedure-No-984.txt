### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Execution:**
- The GroundPeony campaign used a malicious executable named ".exe" or "Install.exe" which was renamed to "mic.exe" for DLL side-loading.
- The side-loaded DLL, "version.dll", functioned as a shellcode launcher for "mic.doc".
- "mic.doc" is a shellcode downloader known as micDown.
- "mic.ver" served as a config file for "mic.doc".

**Defense Evasion:**
- DLL side-loading with "version.dll" to decode and launch shellcode from "mic.doc".
- Two-step custom XOR operations for decoding.
- "mic.doc" payload had its MZ header removed and downloaded encoded shellcode, which it decoded using a similar custom XOR algorithm.

**Command and Control:**
- C&C communication through the domain "app.onedrivo.com" with the IP address 172.93.189.239.
- "mic.ver" config file contained encoded information for connecting to the C&C server, including the IP address and port.

**Others:**
- GroundPeony (UNC3347) is a China-nexus threat group active since 2021, targeting government, research, educational institutes, and telecom sectors in Taiwan, Hong Kong, South Korea, Nepal, and India.
- Linked to past campaigns exploiting the Follina vulnerability (CVE-2022-30190).
- Used infrastructure associated with PublicDomainRegistry, AS63734, AS55720, and AS 30823.
- IoCs include various SHA256 hashes and IP addresses/domains.
