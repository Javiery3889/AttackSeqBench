### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- Spear-phishing campaign targeting US-based retailers.
- Phishing emails contained malicious MS Office documents.
- Used social engineering tactics.
- Leveraged 'Remote Manipulator System' (RMS) to gain entry into networks and evade security controls.

**Persistence:**
- Added a 'Microtik' string value to the 'HKEY_CURRENT_USERS' hive, 'Windows\CurrentVersion\Run' key to launch the RMS executable on user logon.
- RMS executable was launched before the script tried to forcefully kill the 'rundll32.exe' process, causing the script to loop.

**Defense Evasion:**
- Used RMS capabilities for reconnaissance and lateral movement.
- Legitimate remote administration tools were used to avoid detection.

**Command and Control:**
- RMS executable attempted to 'call home' to a C2 server in Germany.
- Linked to TA505 operations using the 'ServHelper' backdoor.

**Others:**
- TA505 has been active since 2014.
- Conducted high-volume malicious email campaigns.
- Distributed banking trojans, botnets, exploit kits, and ransomware.
- Recent campaigns included the use of RMS in attacks against US-based retailers and the financial industry.
- Indicators of compromise consistent with an attack against the Notary Chamber of Ukraine.
