### Step-by-Step Analysis

1. **Analyze the CTI Report:**
   - **Initial Access:**
     - The Adwind RAT is hosted on a compromised Westnet user account.
     - The payload is executed by the victim, involving multiple levels of JAR extractions.
   - **Execution:**
     - The Adwind RAT executes a dropped JAR payload.
     - It creates a parent Java process and copies itself into the %User% directory.
     - It performs three actions:
       1. Executes the copy.
       2. Creates a registry entry in HKCU/CurrentVersion/Run for persistence.
       3. Creates WMI scripts in %temp% to disable firewall and antivirus services.
     - Subsequent JARs perform AES decryption routines and execute as new Java threads, leading to the loading of the JRAT class responsible for major RAT functionality.
   - **Persistence:**
     - The Adwind RAT achieves persistence through registry modifications.
   - **Collection:**
     - The RAT captures webcam images, scans the hard-drive for files, spins up multiple process threads, and performs injection into known legitimate Windows processes.
   - **Command and Control:**
     - The JRAT class within the Adwind RAT tries connecting to its command and control server.
   - **Exfiltration:**
     - The Adwind RAT encrypts and exfiltrates data to its command and control server.

2. **Analyze the Question:**
   - The question asks whether it is likely that the RAT will configure its command and control connection through the JRAT class before creating WMI scripts in the %temp% directory to disable firewall and antivirus services.
