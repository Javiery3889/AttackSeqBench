**Initial Access:**

- Injected into processes like 'rundll32.exe' and 'dllhost.exe'.

**Defense Evasion:**

- Used fileless loading techniques.
- Frequently changed server infrastructure and loader variants.

**Collection:**
- Capabilities include file/directory management, screenshots, file search, process enumeration, file metadata collection, TCP session management, OS information gathering, and location verification.

**Command and Control:**
- Used HTTP and TCP protocols.
- Sent collected data to C&C domain with XORed password.
- Listened for additional commands from the server.

**Others:**
- Targeted national government entities in the Asia Pacific region.
- Expanded footholds by attacking from one compromised entity to another.
- Used infected ministries as C&C servers.
- Infrastructure used GoDaddy for domain registration and Alibaba for hosting.
- Attribution based on targeting, strings, hashing functions, code, and infrastructure overlap.

#### 3. Compare and Evaluate

**Key Points from the CTI Report:**
- The Naikon APT group primarily targeted national government entities in the Asia Pacific region.
- They used a variety of techniques for initial access, execution, persistence, and defense evasion.
- The Aria-body backdoor had extensive capabilities for data collection and C&C communication.
- They used HTTP and TCP protocols for C&C communication.

**Evaluation:**
- The report does not mention any specific targeting or enumeration of AWS services such as CloudTrail and CloudWatch.
- The group's primary targets were national government entities, which are less likely to use AWS services extensively compared to cloud-native or technology companies.
- The Aria-body backdoor's capabilities are focused on file/directory management, screenshots, process enumeration, and OS information gathering, which are more aligned with traditional on-premises or non-cloud environments.
- The group's C&C communication methods (HTTP and TCP) are typical for traditional C&C operations and do not suggest a focus on cloud-based services.
