### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- Spear-phishing campaign targeting US-based retailers.
- Phishing emails contained malicious MS Office documents.
- Used social engineering tactics.
- Leveraged the legitimate remote administration tool 'Remote Manipulator System' (RMS) to gain entry.

**Execution:**
- Malicious MS Office documents instructed victims to disable security controls.
- Macros in the documents downloaded malicious payloads from C2 infrastructure.
- Initial payload was a malware downloader used to gather further components, including RMS, shell scripts, and configuration files.

**Defense Evasion:**
- Threat actors used RMS to conduct reconnaissance and lateral movement.
- Legitimate remote administration tools were used to avoid detection.

**Command and Control:**
- RMS executable attempted to 'call home' to a C2 server in Germany.
- Linked to TA505 operations using the 'ServHelper' backdoor.

**Others:**
- TA505 has been active since 2014.
- Conducted high-volume malicious email campaigns.
- Used RMS in attacks against US-based retailers and the financial industry.
- Indicators of compromise consistent with an attack against the Notary Chamber of Ukraine.

#### 2. Analyze the Question

**Question:**
- After the malicious macro executed from the MS Office documents and successfully downloaded the RMS tool and supporting scripts, is it likely that the threat actors will achieve persistence by using the `sp_addlinkedsrvlogin` command in MS-SQL to create a link between a created account and other servers in the network before attempting to kill the 'rundll32.exe' process?

**Answer Choices:**
- A: Yes
- B: No

#### 3. Compare and Evaluate

**Extracted Attack Sequence:**
1. **Initial Access:** Spear-phishing emails with malicious MS Office documents.
2. **Execution:** Macros in the documents disable security controls and download malicious payloads.
3. **Defense Evasion:** Use of RMS for reconnaissance and lateral movement.

**Question Sequence:**
- After downloading RMS and supporting scripts, will the threat actors use `sp_addlinkedsrvlogin` in MS-SQL to create a link between a created account and other servers in the network before attempting to kill the 'rundll32.exe' process?

**Evaluation:**
- The CTI report does not mention the use of `sp_addlinkedsrvlogin` or any specific SQL commands for persistence.
- The report focuses on the use of RMS for reconnaissance and lateral movement, which aligns with the Defense Evasion and Command and Control tactics.
- The report does not provide any information about the threat actors attempting to kill the 'rundll32.exe' process.
- The use of `sp_addlinkedsrvlogin` for persistence is a specific technique that is not mentioned in the report and does not align with the described attack sequence.

#### 4. Provide a Step-by-Step Reasoning and Final Answer

**Reasoning:**
- The CTI report does not indicate the use of `sp_addlinkedsrvlogin` or any SQL commands for persistence.
- The report focuses on the use of RMS for reconnaissance and lateral movement, which are different from the specific persistence technique mentioned in the question.
