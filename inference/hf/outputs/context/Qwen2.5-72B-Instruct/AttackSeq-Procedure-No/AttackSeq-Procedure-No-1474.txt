### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- The attack starts with the malicious Welcome Chat app, which is distributed via a dedicated website in Arabic.
- The app is linked to the malware named BadPatch and the Gaza Hackers threat actor group (Molerats).

**Persistence:**
- The app auto-starts at device boot by listening for the BOOT_COMPLETED broadcast.

**Collection:**
- The app exfiltrates various data, including SMS messages, call logs, contact lists, user photos, recorded phone calls, and GPS location.

**Command and Control:**
- The app sends device information to its C&C server and is ready to receive commands.
- It contacts the C&C server every five minutes and uploads exfiltrated data using the HTTP protocol.

**Others:**
- The app is a functioning chat app with hidden espionage capabilities.
- It requires intrusive permissions but may seem natural for a messaging app.
- The app's infrastructure is not secure, and harvested data is freely available on the internet.
- The malicious code is copied from publicly available sources.
- The app is believed to be developed by the attackers.
- The campaign is linked to the long-term BadPatch campaign.
