### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- The Duke group used Trojan.Cozer (Cozyduke) to initiate their campaign.
- They sent 'Office Monkeys' and 'eFax'-themed emails containing a Cozyduke payload to compromise high-profile government networks.

**Defense Evasion:**
- Seaduke, a low-profile information-stealing Trojan, uses multiple layers of encryption and obfuscation.
- It is designed to steal and exfiltrate sensitive information such as emails.
- Seaduke communicates with over 200 compromised web servers for command and control.

**Credential Access:**
- The Duke group uses Kerberos pass-the-ticket attacks (Mimikatz PowerShell) and email extraction from the MS Exchange Server using compromised credentials.

**Collection:**
- Seaduke allows attackers to retrieve detailed bot/system information, update bot configuration, upload files, download files, and securely delete the malware using a self-delete function named 'seppuku'.

**Command and Control:**
- Seaduke securely communicates with the command-and-control (C&C) server over HTTP/HTTPS using layers of encoding (Base64) and encryption (RC4, AES).
- The Seaduke control infrastructure is distinct, allowing for sub-teams to concurrently exploit the target network.

**Others:**
- The Duke group has been compromising governmental and diplomatic organizations since at least 2010.
- They have a range of malware tools known as the Dukes, including Cozyduke, Miniduke, and Cosmicduke.
- They have sophisticated operators with fine-tuned computer network exploitation skills.
