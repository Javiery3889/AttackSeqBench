### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- GroundPeony used a zero-day vulnerability (Follina, CVE-2022-30190).
- They distributed malware through spear-phishing emails with lure documents and URL obfuscation.
- Malicious payloads were downloaded and executed from compromised websites.
- Communication was established with a C&C server.

**Execution:**
- A malicious executable (".exe" or "Install.exe") was renamed to "mic.exe".
- "mic.exe" used DLL side-loading with "version.dll".
- "version.dll" functioned as a shellcode launcher for "mic.doc".
- "mic.ver" served as a config file for "mic.doc".
- The attack flow involved executing "mic.exe", loading and decrypting associated files, and communicating with the C&C server.

**Defense Evasion:**
- DLL side-loading was used with "version.dll".
- "version.dll" decoded and launched shellcode from "mic.doc".
- The decoding process involved custom XOR operations.
- "mic.doc" had its MZ header removed and downloaded encoded shellcode, which it decoded using a similar custom XOR algorithm.

**Others:**
- GroundPeony (UNC3347) is a China-nexus threat group targeting government, research, educational, and telecom sectors in East and South Asian countries.
- They have used infrastructure associated with PublicDomainRegistry, AS63734, AS55720, and AS30823.
- IoCs include various SHA256 hashes and IP addresses/domains such as 103.199.17.184, 160.20.145.111, and *.onedrivo.com.
