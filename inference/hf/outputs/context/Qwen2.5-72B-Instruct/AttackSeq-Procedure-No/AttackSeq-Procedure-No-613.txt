### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Resource Development:**
- Bankshot implants are distributed from a domain (falcancoin.io) that resembles the legitimate cryptocurrency-lending platform Falcon Coin.
- The domain was created on December 27, 2017, and updated on February 19, 2018.
- The implants are variations of earlier forms of Bankshot, a remote access tool with destructive capabilities.

**Initial Access:**
- Financial organizations in Turkey were targeted via spear phishing emails containing a malicious Microsoft Word document.
- The document contains an embedded Adobe Flash exploit (CVE-2018-4878) that allows the execution of arbitrary code.

**Execution:**
- The Bankshot implant is attached to a malicious Word document (Agreement.docx) that appears to be an agreement template for Bitcoin distribution.
- The document contains an embedded Flash script that exploits CVE-2018-4878 and downloads and executes the DLL implant from falcancoin.io.

**Persistence:**
- Bankshot is designed to persist on a victim's network for further exploitation.

**Defense Evasion:**
- The implant is designed to evade detection and can wipe files and content to erase evidence.

**Command and Control:**
- The implants communicate with control servers, including Chinese-language online gambling sites.
- The US government reports that Bankshot is used by Hidden Cobra to target multiple industries, including financial organizations.

**Others:**
- The campaign is successful against victims with unpatched Flash.
- Documents with the Flash exploit managed to evade static defenses.
- This is the first time Bankshot has been tied directly to financial-related hacking and the first time it has been used since November 2017.

**Question:**
