### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Execution:**
- The Winnti Group uses a backdoor called skip-2.0.
- The backdoor is launched via a VMProtected launcher.
- Payloads like PortReuse or ShadowPad are associated with the launcher.
- The skip-2.0 backdoor is executed with the SessionEnv service.
- DLL hijacking is used for persistence.

**Defense Evasion:**
- The backdoor employs hooks in the sqllang.dll service to bypass authentication and maintain stealth.
- It blocks event logging.
- The launcher is VMProtected, and the backdoor uses a custom packer for software packing.

**Discovery:**
- The Inner-Loader component lists running processes to find the process running MSSQL Server.

**Impact:**
- The backdoor allows unauthorized access to MSSQL databases.
- It manipulates event logging and stored data, potentially impacting data integrity.

**Others:**
- The Winnti Group is known for high-profile supply-chain attacks.
- The backdoor targets MSSQL Server 11 and 12.
- It uses a 'magic password' to connect stealthily to MSSQL accounts and hides these connections from logs.
- The backdoor shares similarities with PortReuse and ShadowPad.
- ESET has published a white paper with IoCs for skip-2.0.
