- **Defense Evasion**: The report clearly states that the malware is designed to evade detection. This is a key tactic, but it is not the first step in the sequence of events leading to the targeting of corporate employees.
- **Reconnaissance**: This tactic involves gathering information about the target before launching an attack. It is a logical first step before attempting to gain initial access.
- **Initial Access**: This tactic involves gaining entry into the target's network or systems. The report mentions targeting corporate employees and hiring managers with fake job offers and resumes containing malware, which is a form of initial access.
- **Exfiltration**: This tactic involves stealing data from the target. It is typically a later stage in the attack sequence, after gaining access and establishing a foothold.

1. **Defense Evasion**: The malware is designed to evade detection, which is a crucial aspect of the attack but not the first step.
2. **Reconnaissance**: Before targeting specific individuals, the threat actor would need to gather information about the target organization, such as the names and roles of employees and hiring managers. This is a necessary step to craft convincing fake job offers and resumes.
3. **Initial Access**: After gathering the necessary information, the threat actor would use the fake job offers and resumes to gain initial access to the target's systems.
4. **Exfiltration**: This would occur after gaining access and establishing a foothold in the network, which is not mentioned in the report as a step before targeting employees.
