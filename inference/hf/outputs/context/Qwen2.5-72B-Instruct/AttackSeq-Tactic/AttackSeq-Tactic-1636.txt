  - A: Initial Access
  - B: Command and Control

#### 3. Compare and Evaluate
- **Initial Access (A):** This tactic involves gaining initial access to a system, which is typically done through methods like spear-phishing. The report mentions that the actor used spear-phishing emails with URLs linking to RAR files or malicious Microsoft Word attachments to deliver NetTraveler. This is the initial step in the attack sequence.
- **Command and Control (B):** This tactic involves establishing and maintaining control over the compromised system. The report describes the setup of C&C domains and IP addresses, which are used to communicate with the compromised systems. This step logically follows after gaining initial access.
- **Persistence (C):** This tactic involves maintaining long-term access to the compromised system. While the report does not explicitly mention persistence techniques, it is a common next step after establishing C&C.
- **Discovery (D):** This tactic involves the attacker gathering information about the compromised system and network. The report does not provide specific details about discovery activities, but it is typically a step that follows after gaining access and establishing C&C.

- **Step 1:** The actor uses spear-phishing emails to deliver NetTraveler, which is an initial access technique.
