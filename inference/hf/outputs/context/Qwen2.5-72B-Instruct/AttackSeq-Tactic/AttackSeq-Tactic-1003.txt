**Question:**

A: Lateral Movement
B: Persistence

#### 3. Compare and Evaluate

**Initial Access:**
- The report clearly states that the initial access phase involves sending emails mimicking legitimate password recovery or reset requests. This is a common technique for gaining initial access to a target's system.

**Reconnaissance:**
- Reconnaissance involves gathering information about the target before launching an attack. While the report does not explicitly mention reconnaissance, it is a logical step that would occur before sending phishing emails. However, the report focuses on the execution of the attack rather than the preparatory steps.

**Lateral Movement:**
- Lateral movement involves moving through a network to access additional systems. This typically occurs after gaining initial access and is not a prerequisite for sending phishing emails.

**Persistence:**
- Persistence involves maintaining access to a system over an extended period. This is usually a step taken after gaining initial access and is not a prerequisite for sending phishing emails.
