#### 2. Analyze the Question

**Question:**
Which ATT&CK tactic most likely occurred after social engineering was used to trick victims into enabling macros within malicious delivery documents in a campaign targeting organizations in Tajikistan?

**Answer Choices:**
A: Execution
B: Reconnaissance
C: Initial Access
D: Impact

#### 3. Compare and Evaluate

**Initial Access:**
- Social engineering to trick victims into enabling macros is a common method for initial access.
- Once the macros are enabled, the payload is executed, which aligns with the "Execution" tactic.

**Execution:**
- The report clearly states that after enabling the macros, the payload executes, creating files and running scripts.
- This is the immediate next step after initial access.

**Reconnaissance:**
- Reconnaissance typically occurs before the attack to gather information about the target.
- It is not mentioned in the report as a step that occurs after enabling macros.

**Impact:**
- Impact is a later stage in the attack lifecycle, often involving data destruction or disruption.
- The report does not mention any impact actions occurring immediately after enabling macros.
