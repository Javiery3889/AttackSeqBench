### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- Threat actors exploited CVE-2019-0604 to install webshells on the website of a Middle East government organization.
- One of the webshells used is the AntSword webshell.

**Execution:**
- The actors used the AntSword webshell to run commands for initial system and user discovery.
- They also used the webshell to ping systems of interest and upload tools like cURL, a custom Mimikatz variant, and Impacket tools.

**Defense Evasion:**
- The actors used a custom Mimikatz variant with a .NET loader to evade detection.
- They uploaded the Dumpert tool to evade antivirus and EDR solutions.

**Credential Access:**
- The actors used Mimikatz to dump credentials from memory.
- They used Impacket tools to perform pass the hash techniques.

**Lateral Movement:**
- The actors moved laterally to other systems using webshells, Mimikatz, and Impacket's atexec tool.

**Command and Control:**
- The actors used the AntSword webshell to communicate with the compromised server and run commands.
- They also used the AntSword Shell Manager to interact with the webshell.
