### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- Spear-phishing emails with malicious Word, Excel, or PDF attachments.
- Exploits CVE-2017-0199.
- Social engineering content impersonating legitimate companies using typo-squatting domains.
- Template injection to execute macro code, which downloads and executes malware.

**Execution:**
- Malware downloaded are .NET binaries protected with Yoda Obfuscator.
- Commercial RATs like RevengeRAT, NjRAT, NanoCoreRAT, 888 RAT, and ProCC.
- ScreenBooking module captures credit card data by monitoring web pages, specifically targeting booking.com.
- ProCC campaigns use Delphi binaries with a customized backdoor capable of collecting data from the clipboard and printer spooler, as well as capturing screenshots.

**Credential Access:**
- Capture credit card data from hotel systems and online travel agencies.
- Sell credentials from compromised systems to other cybercriminals.
- Provide remote access to hotel front desks infected by the campaign.

**Others:**
- Active since at least 2015.
- Targets multiple countries.
- Uses traditional RAT malware.
- Kaspersky products detect this threat under various names.
- IoCs available on the Kaspersky Threat Intelligence Portal.

#### 2. Analyze the Question

**Question:**
- After capturing credentials from compromised hotel systems and online travel agencies to sell to other cybercriminals, is it likely that the malware used in the RevengeHotels campaign will monitor documents sent to the printer to collect credit card data?

**Answer Choices:**
- A: Yes
- B: No

#### 3. Compare and Evaluate

**Key Points from the CTI Report:**
- The malware includes a module called ScreenBooking that captures credit card data by monitoring web pages, specifically targeting booking.com.
- The ProCC campaigns use a customized backdoor capable of collecting data from the clipboard and printer spooler, as well as capturing screenshots.

**Evaluation:**
- The report explicitly mentions that the ProCC campaigns use a backdoor capable of collecting data from the printer spooler.
- This indicates that the malware is designed to monitor and collect data from documents sent to the printer.
- The primary goal of the campaign is to capture credit card data, and monitoring the printer spooler is a method to achieve this.

#### 4. Provide a Step-by-Step Reasoning and Final Answer

**Reasoning:**
1. The CTI report states that the ProCC campaigns use a backdoor capable of collecting data from the printer spooler.
2. The primary objective of the campaign is to capture credit card data.
3. Monitoring documents sent to the printer is a logical method to collect credit card data, especially in a hotel environment where such documents are frequently printed.

**Final Answer:**
A: Yes
