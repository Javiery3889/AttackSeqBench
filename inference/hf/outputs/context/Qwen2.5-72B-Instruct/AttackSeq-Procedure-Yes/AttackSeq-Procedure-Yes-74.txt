### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- The Adwind RAT is spread through a campaign targeting the petroleum industry in the US.
- The payload is hosted on a compromised Westnet user account.
- The payload involves multiple levels of JAR extractions.

**Execution:**
- The Adwind RAT executes a dropped JAR payload.
- It creates a parent Java process and copies itself into the %User% directory.
- It performs actions such as executing the copy, creating a registry entry for persistence, and creating WMI scripts to disable firewall and antivirus services.
- Subsequent JARs perform AES decryption and execute as new Java threads, leading to the loading of the JRAT class.

**Persistence:**
- The Adwind RAT achieves persistence through registry modifications (HKCU/CurrentVersion/Run).

**Defense Evasion:**
- The RAT uses multi-layer obfuscation techniques, including multiple embedded JAR archives.
- It uses WMI scripts to disable firewall and antivirus services.

**Collection:**
- The RAT captures webcam images, scans the hard drive for files based on extensions, spins up multiple process threads, and performs injection into known legitimate Windows processes.

**Command and Control:**
- The JRAT class within the Adwind RAT tries connecting to its command and control server at 185[.]205[.]210[.]48.
- It encrypts and exfiltrates data to this server.

**Others:**
- The URLs hosting the Adwind RAT were reported to Westnet on September 9th, 2019.
- The VirusTotal detection ratio for the top-level JAR was 5/56, while that of the final decrypted JAR was 49/58.
- The campaign has been active over the last couple of years, with cross-platform functionality for Windows, Linux, and Mac.
