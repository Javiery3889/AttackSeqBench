### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- Mustang Panda initiated phishing campaigns using lures disguised as official European Union reports and fake Ukrainian government reports.
- These lures, when interacted with, downloaded malware onto the victims' machines.

**Execution:**
- Mustang Panda used benign executables to side-load malicious DLLs, which acted as loaders for the PlugX implant.
- They also deployed custom stagers, meterpreter-based shellcode downloaders, and reverse shells to establish long-term persistence.

**Persistence:**
- The group maintained persistence by using benign executables that loaded malicious DLLs, which activated the PlugX RAT implant.
- They also used stagers and reverse shells to ensure continued access.
- Scheduled tasks and registry modifications were employed to maintain their foothold.

**Others:**
- Mustang Panda has been active since at least 2012, targeting various entities worldwide.
- They have evolved their tactics and delivery mechanisms over the years.
