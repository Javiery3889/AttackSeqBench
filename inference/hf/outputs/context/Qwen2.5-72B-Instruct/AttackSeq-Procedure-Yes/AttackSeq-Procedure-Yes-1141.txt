### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Execution:**
- The GroundPeony campaign used a malicious executable named ".exe" or "Install.exe" which was renamed to "mic.exe" for DLL side-loading.
- The side-loaded DLL, "version.dll", functioned as a shellcode launcher for "mic.doc".
- "mic.doc" is a shellcode downloader known as micDown.
- "mic.ver" served as a config file for "mic.doc".
- The attack flow involved executing the renamed executable, loading and decrypting the associated files, and communicating with the C&C server.

**Defense Evasion:**
- DLL side-loading was used with the "version.dll" file to decode and launch shellcode from "mic.doc".
- The decoding process involved custom XOR operations.
- "mic.doc" had its MZ header removed and was responsible for downloading and decoding encoded shellcode.

**Command and Control:**
- Communication with the C&C server was established through the domain "app.onedrivo.com" with the IP address 172.93.189.239.
- The "mic.ver" config file contained encoded information for connecting to the C&C server, including the IP address and port.

**Others:**
- The GroundPeony threat group, also known as UNC3347, is a China-nexus threat group active since at least 2021.
- They target government, research, educational institutes, and telecom sectors in Taiwan, Hong Kong, South Korea, Nepal, and India.
- The group has exploited the Follina vulnerability (CVE-2022-30190) and used infrastructure associated with various companies.
