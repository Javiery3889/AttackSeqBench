### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- Spear-phishing campaign targeting US-based retailers.
- Phishing emails contained malicious MS Office documents.
- Used social engineering tactics.
- Leveraged the legitimate remote administration tool 'Remote Manipulator System' (RMS) to gain entry.

**Execution:**
- Malicious MS Office documents instructed victims to disable security controls.
- Macros executed to download malicious payloads from C2 infrastructure.
- Initial payload was a malware downloader used to gather further components, including RMS, shell scripts, and configuration files.

**Persistence:**
- Second-stage shell script added a 'Microtik' string value to the 'HKEY_CURRENT_USERS' hive, 'Windows\CurrentVersion\Run' key.
- RMS executable launched on user logon.
- Script attempted to forcefully kill the 'rundll32.exe' process, causing it to loop.

**Defense Evasion:**
- RMS tool used for reconnaissance and lateral movement.
- Legitimate remote administration tool capabilities leveraged to avoid detection.

**Others:**
- Threat actor group 'TA505' has been active since 2014.
- Conducted high-volume malicious email campaigns.
- Recent campaigns included the use of RMS in attacks against US-based retailers and the financial industry.
- Indicators of compromise consistent with an attack against the Notary Chamber of Ukraine.
