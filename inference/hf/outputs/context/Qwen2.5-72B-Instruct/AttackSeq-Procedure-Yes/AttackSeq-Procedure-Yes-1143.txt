### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- GroundPeony used a zero-day vulnerability, Follina (CVE-2022-30190), to distribute malware.
- They used spear-phishing emails with lure documents and URL obfuscation to download and execute malicious payloads from compromised websites.
- This led to the establishment of communication with a C&C server.

**Defense Evasion:**
- The campaign used DLL side-loading with the "version.dll" file to decode and launch shellcode from "mic.doc".
- The decoding process involved custom XOR operations.
- "mic.doc" had its MZ header removed and was responsible for downloading and decoding encoded shellcode.

**Command and Control:**
- Communication with the C&C server was established through the domain "app.onedrivo.com" with the IP address 172.93.189.239.
- The "mic.ver" config file contained encoded information for connecting to the C&C server.
