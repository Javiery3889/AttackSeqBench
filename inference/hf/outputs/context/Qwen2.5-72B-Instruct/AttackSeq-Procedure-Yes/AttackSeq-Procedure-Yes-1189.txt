### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- Spear-phishing campaign targeting US-based retailers.
- Phishing emails contained malicious MS Office documents.
- Social engineering tactics were used to trick victims into disabling security controls.
- The malicious documents delivered a macro that would download malicious payloads.

**Execution:**
- The macro disabled security controls and executed to download malicious payloads.
- The initial payload was a malware downloader that gathered further components, including the RMS tool, shell scripts, and configuration files.

**Defense Evasion:**
- The RMS tool was used for reconnaissance and lateral movement.
- The use of a legitimate remote administration tool helped evade detection.

**Command and Control:**
- The RMS executable attempted to connect to a C2 server in Germany.
- Additional C2 domains/IP addresses were linked to TA505 operations using the 'ServHelper' backdoor.

**Others:**
- TA505 has been active since 2014, conducting various malicious campaigns.
- The use of RMS in attacks against US-based retailers and the financial industry.
- Indicators of compromise were consistent with an attack against the Notary Chamber of Ukraine.

#### 2. Analyze the Question

**Question:**
- After the malicious macro executed and downloaded the RMS tool and supporting scripts, is it likely that the threat actors will achieve persistence by adding a 'Microtik' string value to the registry and launching the RMS executable before attempting to kill the 'rundll32.exe' process?

**Answer Choices:**
- A: Yes
