### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Reconnaissance:**
- Charming Kitten targeted non-Iranian researchers and Iranian dissidents.
- They impersonated well-known researchers or journalists.
- They invited recipients to events and used the targets' language.
- Trackers were added to emails to follow forwarded emails and obtain geolocation information.

**Resource Development:**
- Fake websites impersonating Google services (Gmail, Google Drive) and Instagram were used.
- These websites were hosted on servers provided by BelCloud Hosting Corporation in Bulgaria.
- Servers were switched after a month of campaign operations.

**Initial Access:**
- The first stage involved sending socially engineered email messages impersonating known researchers or journalists.
- The second stage redirected victims to decoy websites impersonating Google services and other entities.
- In August 2019, the group expanded to target influential public figures worldwide.

**Defense Evasion:**
- Email trackers were used to monitor forwarded emails and collect geolocation and IP addresses.
- A cookie was installed on the victim's machine to gather data.

**Command and Control:**
- More than six unique servers were used to host fake websites.
- Four servers impersonated entities, and two impersonated Google services.
- These servers were used to control phishing websites and collect stolen data.

**Others:**
- ClearSky Security Ltd. attributed the campaign to Charming Kitten.
- The targeting and impersonation methods fit Charming Kitten's TTPs.
- The NetBlock of the IP addresses linked to an Iranian company.
