### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Initial Access:**
- Exploited public-facing applications (FortiOS CVE-2018-13379, 1-day vulnerabilities, SQL injection).
- Uploaded webshells for lateral movement using RDP.

**Execution:**
- Deployed custom malware, including a .NET backdoor named 'IPsec Helper'.
- 'IPsec Helper' was used to exfiltrate data, deploy additional malware, and execute commands.

**Persistence:**
- Deployed webshells.
- Registered 'IPsec Helper' as a service on compromised systems.

**Defense Evasion:**
- Used webshells with variations of ASPXSpy, obfuscated with a custom base64 encoding function.
- 'IPsec Helper' could update its engine and delete traces of itself.

**Command and Control:**
- 'IPsec Helper' communicated with C2 servers over HTTP.
- Awaited commands for updating relays, sending recon information, downloading files, and self-deletion.

**Impact:**
- Deployed wipers ('Apostle' and DEADWOOD).
- 'Apostle' initially failed but was later turned into ransomware.
- DEADWOOD overwrote files with random data and had functionalities to prevent user access.
