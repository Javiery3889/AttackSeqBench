### Step-by-Step Analysis

#### 1. Analyze the CTI Report

**Execution:**
- Shuckworm executed VBS scripts and HTA files to maintain their presence and execute their backdoor.
- Created scheduled tasks to ensure persistence.
- Executed commands via scripts.
- Additional variants of Pterodo were executed on July 16.
- A VNC client was installed as the ultimate payload on July 28.

**Persistence:**
- Established persistence by creating scheduled tasks that executed malicious VBS files every few minutes.
- Ensured the backdoor remained active after system reboots.

**Defense Evasion:**
- Utilized mshta.exe to bypass application control solutions and browser security settings.
- Used scheduled tasks and VBS scripts to evade detection and maintain their foothold.

**Collection:**
- Deployed multiple variants of their custom VBS backdoor and executed VBS scripts between July 29 and August 18.
- Unclear if documents accessed were part of collection efforts.

**Command and Control:**
- Established command and control by communicating with servers using HTTP requests.
- Downloaded additional payloads from C&C servers (deep-pitched.enarto.ru, arianat.ru, mucoris.ru).

**Others:**
- Symantec's Threat Hunter Team has been tracking Shuckworm's activities.
- Shuckworm has been active since 2013 and has increased sophistication.
- SSU reported Shuckworm using living-off-the-land tools to steal credentials and move laterally.
- Seven files used by Shuckworm, including VBS files, VNC client, and Process Explorer binary.
- IOCs showed patterns of C&C IPs and malicious files in specific directories.
- SSU attributed over 5,000 attacks to Shuckworm since 2014, targeting Ukrainian government systems.
