- Reconnaissance: The outline starts with Hidden Cobra targeting cryptocurrency and financial organizations, specifically in the Turkish financial system with Bankshot malware.
- Initial Access: Financial organizations in Turkey were targeted via spear phishing emails with a malicious Microsoft Word document containing an Adobe Flash exploit (CVE-2018-4878).
- Execution: The Bankshot implant is attached to the malicious Word document and uses the Flash exploit to download and execute the DLL implant.
- Persistence: Bankshot is designed to persist on the network for further exploitation.
- Defense Evasion: The implant contains functionality to wipe files and content to evade detection.
- Command and Control: The implants communicate with hardcoded control servers, and the implant is known for targeting the SWIFT network.
- Others: The campaign has a high chance of success against unpatched Flash versions, and the malware is detected by McAfee with various threat names.

The attack sequence in the order presented by the MITRE ATT&CK tactics is:
1. Initial Access
2. Execution
3. Persistence
4. Defense Evasion
5. Command and Control
